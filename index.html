What's older than written language?

Signage. 

         Although this term was only recently popularized in the late 70's, for nearly 40,000 years Humans have been using signs and symbols in place of words to identify the presence of someone, somewhere, or something. From hand-drawn cave paintings to wayfinding and advertisment, warnings and regulations, signs have evolved to suit modern taste in-tune with society.

         Bylaws, regulations, innovation, and imagination have all influenced not only the design but also the fabrication methods and techniques used to produce a better, safer, more durable product. Heck, during the reasearch for this project, we learned that one of the the first bylaws for signs was written in France. It addressed Horseman stiking their heads on signs that were hanging over sidewalks. The Result? Signs could no longer hang over the sidewalk. In fact, these signs had to be fixed flat to a wall or protruding no further than 4 inches. When the neighbourhood became saturated with signboards, they needed to distinguish whos pub was whos, and started to use address numbers and street signs. Who Knew?

Here at The Sign Bible, you can aquire the knowledge (or at least know where to find it) on How to Design, How to Build, and How to Service some examples of todays signage. Learn about the history of signs, construction materials and tools, fabrication techniques, installation tips, and common electrical sign troubleshooting.
